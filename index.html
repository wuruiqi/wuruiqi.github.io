I am a Lecturer and M.S. supervisor at Henan University of Technology, working on intelligent perception & decision-making and embodied AI. The core question I care about: how can a robot build, in real time, an understanding of the 3D world and act on its own — in real environments without a prior map and under changing lighting and dynamic scenes? My technical path centers on visual SLAM, 3D scene reconstruction, and 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS), currently carried by indoor scene perception for biped service robots and validated in real settings such as smart grain silos. During my PhD I worked extensively on robotic calligraphy and artistic creation/generation, with results in IEEE TII (JCR Q1, Top), Neurocomputing, and Information Sciences; in recent years I have moved toward broader embodied perception and 3D reconstruction. I have led a Henan Provincial Natural Science Foundation (Youth) project, a Henan Provincial Key Science & Technology project, an open project of the MOE Key Laboratory of Grain Information Processing & Control, a Henan key research project for higher-education institutions, and others, with 10+ publications in total. I serve as a reviewer for IEEE TIP, IEEE TCDS, Knowledge-Based Systems, and other journals.

Prospective students

My direction is intelligent perception & decision-making and embodied AI. The core question: let a robot build, in real time, an understanding of the 3D world — without a prior map and under changing lighting and dynamic scenes — and act on it. Our main technical lines are visual SLAM, 3D scene reconstruction, and 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS), currently carried by indoor scene perception for biped service robots and validated in real settings such as smart grain silos. In one line: we make robots truly understand the 3D world and act on it — the methods should reach good journals, and the systems should run on real hardware.

The way we work may differ from what you expect: we make full use of modern methods and tools to assist research — surveying literature, clarifying problems, speeding up experiments — to free people from repetitive labor and focus on the parts that truly need judgment and creativity. This also means I do not require your coding or English to be strong right now; those barriers are dropping fast today. What really decides how far you go is the ability to dig in independently and learn quickly. This is more of a small team building things side by side than a desk that hands out tasks. I mentor you one-on-one and won't dump you into a pile of chores.

If you join, you will get:

  • Full, end-to-end involvement in real, frontier topics — from problem selection and attack to publication, with you as the lead;
  • One-on-one, hands-on mentoring and a modern research methodology that saves you a couple of years of detours;
  • A stable research stipend, and support to attend academic conferences at home and abroad;
  • Connections in academia and industry — whether you later aim for a PhD, study abroad, a top tech company, or a firm, I'll do my best to introduce and help.

What I truly value is someone who:

  • Has the drive to research independently and gnaw a problem down yourself — when stuck, your first instinct is to find a way, not wait to be spoon-fed;
  • Learns fast and digests new knowledge and tools — asking, judging, and getting hands-on matters more than "already knowing";
  • Has a geek's hands-on ability — likes to tinker, and is willing to actually build ideas, run them, and see results;
  • Is curious about unsolved problems and willing to treat graduate study as something worth a few serious years.

Coding and English? A foundation is nice, but don't be anxious if you lack it — as long as you're willing to learn and get hands-on, I'll help you catch up. Frankly, attitude and the drive to dig in are what I value most; the tools we practice together.

An honest note: if your main energy during graduate study will go to side jobs or preparing for civil-service / public- institution exams, we may not be the best match — nothing to do with how capable you are, just a difference in direction, and saying so up front is fair to us both. But if you genuinely want to build something — even from an ordinary starting point — you're welcome to talk with me.

Interested? Email rqwu@haut.edu.cn (Ruiqi Wu) early — a brief note about yourself, why you want to pursue graduate study, and your plans; nothing too formal. Slots are limited each year — looking forward to meeting you.

招生说明

我的研究方向是智能感知与决策、具身智能，核心问题是：让机器人在没有先验地图、光照和场景不断变化的真实环境中， 实时建立起对三维世界的理解，并据此自主行动。围绕这个问题，我们的技术主线包括视觉 SLAM、三维场景重建与三维高斯泼溅（3DGS）， 当前以双足服务机器人的室内场景感知为主要载体，并在智慧粮仓等真实场景中验证落地。一句话：我们做的是让机器人真正 “看懂”三维世界并行动起来——方法要发得了好期刊，系统也要在真机上跑得起来。

我们的做事方式可能和你想象的不太一样：我们会充分利用各种方法和工具来辅助科研——帮助梳理文献、厘清问题、加速实验验证，把人从重复劳动里解放出来， 更专注于真正需要判断和创造的环节。这也意味着我并不要求你现在的编程或英语有多强，这些门槛在今天正在快速降低； 真正决定你能走多远的，是独立钻研和快速学习的能力。这里更像一个并肩做事的小团队，而不是发任务的工位——我会一对一地带你，不会把你丢进一堆杂活里。

如果你加入，你会得到：

  • 真实、前沿课题的全程参与——从选题、攻关到发表，你是主角；
  • 一对一、手把手的指导，以及一套现代化的科研方法，让你少走两年弯路；
  • 稳定的科研补助，以及资助参加国内外学术会议；
  • 我在学术界和业界有一定的人脉积累，无论你将来想读博、出国深造、进大厂还是去企业，都会尽力为你引荐、提供一些帮助。

我真正看重的，是这样的你：

  • 有独立钻研、自己把问题啃下来的劲头——遇到难题，第一反应是想办法，而不是等人喂答案；
  • 能快速学习、消化新知识和新工具——会问、会判断、会上手，比“早就懂”更重要；
  • 有极客式的动手能力——喜欢折腾，愿意把想法真的做出来、跑起来、看到结果；
  • 对没解决的问题有好奇心，愿意把读研当成一件值得认真投入几年的事。

编程和英语？有基础当然好，没有也不必焦虑——只要你愿意学、肯上手，我会带你补上。说白了，态度和钻研的劲头我最看重，工具我们一起练。

也跟你说句实话：如果你读研的主要精力打算放在兼职或备考公务员、事业编上，那我们可能不是最合适的搭配——这无关优秀与否，只是方向不同， 提前讲清楚，对彼此都好。但只要你真心想做出点东西，哪怕起点普通，我都欢迎你来聊聊。
